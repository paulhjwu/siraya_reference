# Siraya pronoun system

Source: Adelaar (2011), *Siraya: Retrieving the Phonology, Grammar and Lexicon of a Dormant Formosan Language*, Table 2 & Table 3.

Siraya pronouns distinguish four series — **independent**, **nominative**, **genitive**, and **oblique** — across seven person/number categories. The 1st person plural additionally distinguishes **inclusive** (speaker + addressee) from **exclusive** (speaker, not addressee).

---

## Pronoun paradigm

| Person | Number | Clusivity | Independent | Nominative | Genitive | Oblique |
|--------|--------|-----------|-------------|-----------|---------|---------|
| 1st | singular | — | `ĭau` | `=ko` | `=(m)au` | `ĭau-an` |
| 2nd | singular | — | `ĭmhu` | `=kaw` | `=oho` / `=(m)hu` | `ĭmhu-an` |
| 3rd | singular | — | `teni` | `ta teni` | `tin` | `tini-än` |
| 1st | plural | inclusive | `ĭmĭta` | `=kĭta` | `=eta` / `=mĭta` | `ĭmitä-n` |
| 1st | plural | exclusive | `ĭmian` | `=kame` | `=(m)ian` / `=(m)iän` | `ĭmian-än` |
| 2nd | plural | — | `ĭmumi` | `=kamu` | `=(m)umi` | `ĭmumi-än` |
| 3rd | plural | — | `neini` | `ta neini` | `nein` | `neini-än` |

---

## Series descriptions

**Independent** pronouns are freestanding emphatic forms; they introduce or stress the person referred to. They are case-neutral and do not cliticise to the verb.

**Nominative** pronouns mark the syntactic subject of the clause. Most are enclitics attaching to the verb (`=ko`, `=kaw`, `=kame`, etc.); the 3rd person forms are free (`ta teni`, `ta neini`), using the nominative marker `ta` plus the independent pronoun.

**Genitive** pronouns mark the actor when not in subject position, or the possessor of a noun. They are enclitics for all persons except 3rd singular (`tin`) and 3rd plural (`nein`), which are free forms. Several persons have two alternants: the `(m)` element in `=(m)au`, `=(m)hu`, `=(m)ian`, `=(m)umi` is an optional consonant that appears in certain phonological environments.

**Oblique** pronouns cover all remaining semantic roles (recipient, beneficiary, location, instrument, etc.). They are free forms built from the independent pronoun plus the suffix `-an` / `-än`: e.g. `ĭau` → `ĭau-an`, `teni` → `tini-än`. The 3rd singular oblique `tini-än` is the most frequently attested form in the Gospel texts.

---

## Notes

- The 1st person plural **inclusive** (`ĭmĭta` / `=kĭta`) includes both the speaker and the addressee ("we, including you").
- The 1st person plural **exclusive** (`ĭmian` / `=kame`) includes the speaker but excludes the addressee ("we, not you").
- Nominative and genitive forms of 3rd person are **free** (not clitics), unlike all other persons.
- The genitive `ki` (default case marker) is used for common nouns, not personal pronouns.
- Abbreviations: `1s` = 1st sg · `2s` = 2nd sg · `3s` = 3rd sg · `1pi` = 1st pl inclusive · `1pe` = 1st pl exclusive · `2p` = 2nd pl · `3p` = 3rd pl
