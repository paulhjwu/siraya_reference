# Siraya grammatical categories

Based on Adelaar (2011) and everything extracted from the texts, Siraya has the following grammatical categories.

---

## Verbal categories (expressed by affixes or clitics on the verb)

1. **Voice** — actor-oriented vs. undergoer-oriented (the structural core of the grammar)
2. **Verb class** — classes 1, 2, 3, 4 (determines which affixes are available)
3. **Tense** — past (`ni-`) vs. non-past (unmarked)
4. **Aspect** — perfective (`=ato`), progressive (Ca-reduplication)
5. **Mood** — indicative, subjunctive (`-a`, `-ey`, `-aw`, `-aney`), imperative (`-i`, `-u`)
6. **Causativity** — causative (`pa-`, `pa-ka-`, `pa-pa-`)
7. **Inchoativity** — "become X" (`mi-`, `käwx-`)

---

## Nominal categories

8. **Case** — nominative, genitive, oblique, default, locative (expressed by free case markers `ta`, `ki`, `tu` and suffix `-än`)
9. **Deixis** — proximal vs. distal (`ăta` / `ăna`)
10. **Number/collectivity** — collective suffix `-an`; inclusive circumfix `ka-…-an`
11. **Possession** — genitive clitics and possessive `a=`

---

## Person & reference

12. **Person** — 1st, 2nd, 3rd (via pronominal clitics)
13. **Number** — singular, plural (via pronominal clitics)
14. **Clusivity** — inclusive vs. exclusive 1st person plural

---

## Derivational categories (change word class or meaning)

15. **Nominalisation** — multiple strategies (Ca-reduplication, `-ǝn`/`-an`, circumfixes, `tama-`)
16. **Orientation** — motion (`u-`/`äw-`), location (`i-`/`ĭ-`), comitative (`a-`/`ä-`)
17. **Inversive** — "undo/reverse" (`aäw-`/`ääw-`)
18. **Transformative** — "turn X into Y" (`paha-`)

---

## Discourse & clause-level categories

19. **Negation** — declarative (`ăsi`), prohibitive (`ĭna`), existential (`ausi`)
20. **Polarity** — positive (`akumea`) vs. negative (`ausi`) existential
21. **Evidentiality / modality** — epistemic (`lava` "perhaps"), assertive (`misĭng` "certainly"), deontic (`kidi` "ought to")
22. **Additivity** — `=ăpa` "also, too"
23. **Adversativity** — `=da`/`=ra`/`nda` "but, however"
24. **Linkage** — clause coordination and subordination (`ka`/`k'=`)

---

## Summary count

| Domain | Categories |
|--------|-----------|
| Verbal (inflectional) | 7 |
| Nominal | 4 |
| Person & reference | 3 |
| Derivational | 4 |
| Discourse & clause-level | 6 |
| **Total** | **24** |

---

A few caveats worth noting. The boundaries between some categories are blurry in Siraya — for instance, **voice** and **case** are deeply interdependent (the voice of the verb determines which argument surfaces as nominative), so some analyses treat them as a single "symmetrical voice" system rather than two separate categories. Similarly, **verb class** could be seen as a sub-feature of voice rather than an independent category. Depending on how finely or broadly you draw the lines, a reasonable analysis could arrive at anywhere from around 18 to 26 distinct grammatical categories.
